# Funding your Ergo project

There are multiple ways to secure funding for work in the Ergo ecosystem. Choose the path that best fits your project’s size, scope, and timeline.

## At a glance

- Crowdfunding: Launch an ErgoRaffle to validate community support and raise funds
  - Learn more: [ergoraffle.com](https://ergoraffle.com) and [ErgoRaffle](ergoraffle.md)
- Grants: Apply to community grant programs or email a proposal
  - [Good Whale Fund](https://github.com/ergoplatform/grow-ergo/issues/13)
  - [DarkFund0](https://github.com/ergoplatform/grow-ergo/issues/1)
  - Direct proposal: [team@ergoplatform.org](mailto:team@ergoplatform.org)
- Bounties and rewards: Contribute to open tasks and claim a reward
  - Overview and how to claim: [Bounties](bounties.md)
  - Development rewards: [ErgoDevs Ergo-Bounties](https://github.com/ErgoDevs/Ergo-Bounties)
- Community funding rails:
  - [Mew Finance](mew-finance.md): centralized and flexible options
  - [Bene](bene.md): peer‑to‑peer, smart‑contract based; no backend required

## Recommended first step: Crowdfund with ErgoRaffle

A raffle is often the fastest way to:

- Validate that the community supports your idea
- Set a clear funding goal and timeline
- Combine community support with potential grants later

Where to start:

- Platform: [ergoraffle.com](https://ergoraffle.com/)
- Overview: [ErgoRaffle](ergoraffle.md)

Basic approach:

1. Define your goal, deliverables, and timeline.
2. Create a raffle with an appealing prize or supporter perks.
3. Set contribution parameters and percentage allocations as needed.
4. Share your campaign and collect community backing.

## Apply for grants

For larger initiatives, apply to community-managed funds:

- [Good Whale Fund](https://github.com/ergoplatform/grow-ergo/issues/13)
- [DarkFund0](https://github.com/ergoplatform/grow-ergo/issues/1)

If your project has unique needs or doesn’t fit neatly into existing programs, you can email a proposal:

- [team@ergoplatform.org](mailto:team@ergoplatform.org)

Tips for a strong proposal:

- Problem statement and impact
- Scope, milestones, and success criteria
- Budget and timeline
- Maintenance plan (if applicable)
- Links to prior work or references

## Earn through bounties and reward programs

Bounties are available for both educational content and development work.

- Overview and process: [Bounties](bounties.md)
- Development Reward Program (significant contributions to Ergo repos):
  - [ErgoDevs Ergo-Bounties](https://github.com/ErgoDevs/Ergo-Bounties)
- Educational Reward Program (tutorials, guides, and learning materials):
  - Submit high-quality materials; even items not explicitly listed may be eligible.

How to claim a bounty or reward:

1. Email [team@ergoplatform.org](mailto:team@ergoplatform.org) with your claim.
2. Include where the bounty was posted (or reference [Bounties](bounties.md)).
3. Attach evidence (links, PRs, files, write‑ups).
4. Include your Ergo address for payment.
5. Optional but recommended: include estimated time spent to help expedite review.

## Additional funding rails

- [Mew Finance](mew-finance.md): Centralized but powerful, offering flexible options for project support.
- [Bene](bene.md): Smart‑contract, peer‑to‑peer funding (no backend required). Currently supports ERG exchanged for a custom token (not, e.g., SigUSD).

## Need help?

If you are unsure which path fits your project:

- Start with an ErgoRaffle to gauge support, then supplement with a grant application.
- Reach out with questions or proposals: [team@ergoplatform.org](mailto:team@ergoplatform.org)
